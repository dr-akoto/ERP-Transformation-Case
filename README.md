# Alpha Manufacturing Group (AMG) ERP Transformation Case

## Overview

This repository contains the deliverables for the **Alpha Manufacturing Group (AMG) ERP Transformation Case Study**. The objective is to present a practical ERP transformation strategy that addresses operational challenges, improves business performance, and supports the successful replacement of AMG's legacy Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system.

The submission consists of an executive memorandum and a presentation developed for AMG's Executive Leadership Team and Board of Directors. Both deliverables provide strategic recommendations on programme governance, implementation sequencing, scope definition, data migration, and reporting continuity while balancing business value with implementation risk.

---

## Business Context

Alpha Manufacturing Group (AMG) is a mid-sized North American industrial equipment manufacturer operating across the United States and Canada.

**Company Profile**

- Approximately 200 employees
- Annual revenue of approximately USD 100 million
- Five operating locations across the United States and Canada
- Engineer-to-order manufacturing business
- Aftermarket parts and service operations
- Multi-entity organisational structure

Due to the customised nature of customer orders, accurate engineering data, Bill of Materials (BOM) management, and real-time operational visibility are essential for maintaining profitability and operational efficiency.

---

## Current Business Challenges

AMG currently operates within a fragmented application landscape that limits operational performance and financial visibility.

### Existing Environment

- Legacy ERP supporting Finance, Manufacturing, Material Requirements Planning (MRP), and Procurement
- Independent Engineering system managing Bills of Materials (BOMs) and Engineering Change Notices (ECNs)
- Stand-alone Cloud CRM
- Independent HRIS and Payroll platform
- Extensive reliance on Microsoft Excel for reporting and analysis

### Business Impact

The current environment has contributed to several operational and financial challenges, including:

- Margin erosion across customised customer orders
- Limited contribution margin visibility
- Inaccurate labour cost tracking
- Manufacturing delays caused by BOM synchronisation failures
- Fragmented reporting across business functions
- Inconsistent operational data for management decision-making

---

## Transformation Objectives

The proposed ERP transformation programme is designed to achieve the following objectives:

- Replace the legacy ERP platform with a modern cloud-based solution.
- Integrate core business functions into a single enterprise platform.
- Improve operational visibility and financial reporting.
- Strengthen data governance and master data quality.
- Reduce operational risk during implementation.
- Support long-term business growth through scalable digital capabilities.

---

## Deliverables

This repository contains the following documents:

- **Executive Memorandum** – A strategic recommendation outlining the proposed ERP implementation approach, governance framework, implementation scope, risk management strategy, and data continuity plan.

- **Presentation Slide Deck** – An executive presentation summarising the key recommendations, implementation roadmap, programme risks, scope decisions, and expected business outcomes.

---

## Key Recommendations

The proposed implementation strategy focuses on:

- A twelve-month ERP transformation programme.
- A single integrated ERP core for Finance, Manufacturing, Procurement, MRP, and Engineering.
- Phased implementation with non-critical systems deferred to Phase Two.
- Strong programme governance and executive oversight.
- Disciplined scope and change management.
- Comprehensive data migration and reporting continuity planning.
- Parallel financial validation before production deployment.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Successful implementation will enable AMG to:

- Improve contribution margin visibility.
- Strengthen financial governance and reporting accuracy.
- Integrate Engineering and Manufacturing processes.
- Improve operational efficiency and production planning.
- Reduce implementation and operational risk.
- Establish a scalable digital foundation for future growth.
